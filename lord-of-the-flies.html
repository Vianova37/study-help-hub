🏝️ Lord of the Flies

Lord of the Flies Study Guide

Summary, characters, themes, symbols, quotes, essay help, and quiz.

⭐ Overview

Author: William Golding

Published: 1954

Genre: Allegorical Novel

Setting: A deserted tropical island during a wartime evacuation.

Main Idea: A group of boys stranded on an island attempt to govern themselves. As fear and the desire for power grow, civilization breaks down and violence takes over.

👥 Main Characters

Ralph
The elected leader who represents order, responsibility, and civilization.

Jack Merridew
Leader of the choir boys who becomes obsessed with hunting and power.

Piggy
The intelligent boy who represents logic, science, and reason.

Simon
A kind and thoughtful boy who understands the true nature of the "beast."

Roger
A cruel boy who enjoys hurting others and becomes increasingly violent.

Sam and Eric (Samneric)
Twin brothers who remain loyal to Ralph until they are forced to join Jack.

📖 Story Summary (Part 1)

A plane crashes on a deserted island, leaving a group of British schoolboys stranded with no adults.

Ralph is elected chief, while Jack is disappointed that he loses the election. Piggy helps Ralph organize the boys using a conch shell to call meetings.

The boys decide their main goal is to keep a signal fire burning so passing ships can rescue them.

Jack becomes obsessed with hunting pigs instead of helping maintain the signal fire. The boys begin to fear a mysterious "beast" living on the island.

📖 Story Summary (Part 2)

Fear spreads among the boys, allowing Jack to gain more power by promising protection from the beast.

Simon discovers that the beast is actually a dead parachutist and realizes the real evil comes from within the boys themselves.

During a violent dance, the boys mistake Simon for the beast and accidentally kill him.

Jack's tribe steals Piggy's glasses to make fire. Later, Roger drops a huge boulder that kills Piggy and destroys the conch shell.

Jack hunts Ralph across the island. Just as Ralph is about to be killed, a naval officer arrives and rescues the boys.

🎭 Major Themes

  • Civilization vs. Savagery
  • Power
  • Fear
  • Human Nature
  • Loss of Innocence
  • Leadership
  • Violence

🎭 Important Symbols

The Conch Shell
Represents order, rules, leadership, and civilization.

Piggy's Glasses
Represent intelligence, science, and the ability to create fire.

The Beast
Represents fear and the evil inside human nature.

The Signal Fire
Represents hope, rescue, and connection to civilization.

The Lord of the Flies
Represents evil, corruption, and savagery.

💬 Important Quotes

"Maybe there is a beast... maybe it's only us."

Meaning: Simon realizes that the real evil is inside the boys themselves.

"The thing is — fear can't hurt you any more than a dream."

Meaning: Ralph tries to use reason against fear.

"Which is better — to have rules and agree, or to hunt and kill?"

Meaning: Piggy questions whether civilization or violence should guide them.

📝 Essay Topics

  • How does the conch symbolize civilization?
  • How does fear affect the boys on the island?
  • Compare Ralph and Jack as leaders.
  • What does Simon understand about the beast?
  • How does the novel show the loss of innocence?

⭐ Must Know Before the Test

  • Ralph represents order and leadership.
  • Jack represents power and savagery.
  • Piggy represents logic and intelligence.
  • Simon understands that the beast is inside the boys.
  • The conch loses power as civilization breaks down.
  • Piggy dies and the conch is destroyed at the same time.
  • The naval officer rescues the boys at the end.

📇 Quick Flashcards

Q: What does the conch symbolize?
A: Order, rules, and civilization.

Q: What do Piggy's glasses symbolize?
A: Intelligence, science, and fire.

Q: Who represents savagery?
A: Jack.

Q: What is the beast really?
A: The evil and fear inside humans.
