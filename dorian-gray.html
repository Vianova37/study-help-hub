🎨 The Picture of Dorian Gray

The Picture of Dorian Gray Study Guide

Summary, characters, themes, symbols, quotes, essay help, and quiz.

⭐ Overview

Author: Oscar Wilde

Published: 1890

Genre: Gothic Fiction, Philosophical Novel

Setting: London, England

Main Idea: Dorian Gray wishes to stay young forever while a portrait ages in his place. As he commits increasingly immoral acts, the portrait reflects the corruption of his soul, exploring vanity, temptation, and the consequences of one's choices.

👥 Main Characters

Dorian Gray
A handsome young man whose portrait ages instead of him after he makes a wish to remain forever young.

Lord Henry Wotton
A wealthy and persuasive man whose ideas about pleasure and beauty strongly influence Dorian.

Basil Hallward
A talented artist who paints Dorian's portrait and genuinely cares about him.

Sibyl Vane
A young actress whom Dorian falls in love with before cruelly rejecting her.

James Vane
Sibyl's protective brother who later seeks revenge against Dorian.

📖 Story Summary (Part 1)

Basil Hallward paints a portrait of the handsome young Dorian Gray. Lord Henry Wotton introduces Dorian to the idea that youth and beauty are the most valuable things in life.

Dorian becomes obsessed with staying young forever. Looking at the finished portrait, he wishes that he could remain young while the portrait grows old instead.

His wish mysteriously comes true.

Dorian falls in love with the actress Sibyl Vane. However, after she performs badly because she is distracted by her love for him, Dorian cruelly rejects her.

Heartbroken, Sibyl dies. When Dorian returns home, he notices that the portrait has changed for the first time. His face remains perfect, but the portrait now shows cruelty.

📖 Story Summary (Part 2)

Over many years, Dorian lives a selfish and sinful life while never aging. The portrait becomes uglier with every cruel action he commits.

Basil confronts Dorian after hearing rumors about him. Dorian reveals the portrait, and Basil is horrified by what it has become.

In a moment of panic and anger, Dorian murders Basil.

Eventually, Dorian decides to destroy the portrait with the same knife he used to kill Basil. Instead, he kills himself. Servants later discover an old, ugly man lying dead beside a beautiful portrait of young Dorian Gray.

🎭 Major Themes

  • Beauty vs. Morality
  • Temptation
  • Corruption
  • Influence
  • Vanity
  • Consequences of Sin
  • The Nature of the Soul

🎭 Important Symbols

The Portrait
Represents Dorian's soul. While Dorian stays physically young, the portrait reveals every sin and immoral choice he makes.

The Yellow Book
Represents temptation and Lord Henry's influence over Dorian.

The Mirror
Represents vanity and Dorian's obsession with his appearance.

The Knife
Represents guilt, violence, and Dorian's attempt to destroy his conscience.

💬 Important Quotes

"The only way to get rid of a temptation is to yield to it."

Meaning: Lord Henry believes people should follow every desire.

"Each of us has Heaven and Hell in him."

Meaning: Every person has both good and evil within them.

"Behind every exquisite thing that existed, there was something tragic."

Meaning: Beauty often hides pain or suffering.

"To cure the soul by means of the senses, and the senses by means of the soul."

📝 Essay Topics

  • How does Lord Henry influence Dorian?
  • Explain the symbolism of the portrait.
  • Is Dorian responsible for his own downfall?
  • Compare Basil and Lord Henry's influence on Dorian.
  • How does Oscar Wilde explore beauty and morality?

⭐ Must Know Before the Test

  • The portrait represents Dorian's soul.
  • Lord Henry influences Dorian's way of thinking.
  • Basil represents morality and conscience.
  • Dorian murders Basil.
  • Sibyl dies after Dorian rejects her.
  • Dorian dies after stabbing the portrait.
  • The novel warns against vanity and living without consequences.
