📖 The Great Gatsby

The Great Gatsby Study Guide

Full summary with spoilers, characters, themes, symbols, quotes, and practice questions.

⭐ Overview

Author: F. Scott Fitzgerald

Published: 1925

Genre: Novel, tragedy, social criticism

Setting: New York and Long Island during the 1920s

Main idea: The novel shows how wealth, obsession, and the American Dream can become corrupted.

📖 Full Summary With Spoilers

The story is narrated by Nick Carraway, who moves to West Egg, Long Island. His neighbor is Jay Gatsby, a mysterious millionaire who throws huge parties every weekend.

Nick learns that Gatsby is deeply in love with Daisy Buchanan, Nick’s cousin. Daisy is married to Tom Buchanan, a rich and arrogant man who is also having an affair with Myrtle Wilson.

Gatsby became rich because he wanted to impress Daisy and win her back. He believes he can recreate the past and make Daisy love him the way she once did.

Nick helps Gatsby and Daisy meet again. They begin an affair, but Daisy is unsure whether she can leave Tom. During a confrontation in New York, Tom exposes Gatsby’s shady background and forces Daisy to choose.

Later, Daisy accidentally hits and kills Myrtle while driving Gatsby’s car. Gatsby takes the blame because he still loves Daisy.

Myrtle’s husband, George Wilson, believes Gatsby is responsible. He kills Gatsby and then kills himself. Almost nobody attends Gatsby’s funeral.

By the end, Nick realizes that Gatsby’s dream was based on illusion. He becomes disgusted by the careless behavior of wealthy people like Tom and Daisy.

👥 Main Characters

Jay Gatsby
A mysterious millionaire who throws extravagant parties. Everything he does is motivated by his love for Daisy and his dream of recreating the past.

Nick Carraway
The narrator of the novel. He is honest, thoughtful, and slowly realizes how careless the wealthy people around him really are.

Daisy Buchanan
Beautiful, charming, and Gatsby's former love. She represents Gatsby's dream but ultimately chooses security over true love.

Tom Buchanan
Daisy's wealthy husband. Arrogant, controlling, and unfaithful. He represents old money and privilege.

Jordan Baker
A professional golfer and Daisy's friend. She begins a relationship with Nick.

George Wilson
A hardworking mechanic whose life falls apart after Myrtle's death.

Myrtle Wilson
George's wife and Tom's mistress. She dreams of escaping poverty.

💡 Major Themes

  • The American Dream
  • Love and Obsession
  • Wealth and Social Class
  • The Past vs. Reality
  • Appearance vs. Truth
  • Loneliness
  • Corruption of Wealth

🎭 Important Symbols

The Green Light
Represents Gatsby's hopes, dreams, and his desire for Daisy.

The Valley of Ashes
Represents poverty, decay, and the consequences of wealth.

The Eyes of Doctor T. J. Eckleburg
Often interpreted as representing judgment or the loss of moral values.

Gatsby's Mansion
Represents wealth, success, and Gatsby's attempt to impress Daisy.

💬 Important Quotes

"So we beat on, boats against the current, borne back ceaselessly into the past."

"Can't repeat the past?... Why of course you can!"

"They're a rotten crowd... You're worth the whole damn bunch put together."

📝 Essay Topics

  • Is Gatsby truly great?
  • How does Fitzgerald criticize the American Dream?
  • Explain the symbolism of the green light.
  • How does wealth influence the characters?
  • Discuss the relationship between Gatsby and Daisy.

❓ Practice Questions

  1. Why does Gatsby throw lavish parties?
  2. Why does Nick admire Gatsby despite his flaws?
  3. What does the green light symbolize?
  4. Why doesn't Daisy leave Tom?
  5. Who kills Gatsby, and why?
  6. What lesson does the novel teach about the American Dream?
