🐷 Animal Farm

Animal Farm Study Guide

Summary, characters, themes, symbols, essay topics, and quiz.

⭐ Overview

Author: George Orwell

Published: 1945

Genre: Allegory, satire, political fiction

Main idea: The story shows how power can corrupt leaders and how revolutions can be betrayed.

📖 Full Summary With Spoilers

The animals on Manor Farm are mistreated by Mr. Jones. Old Major inspires them with the idea that animals should be free and equal.

After Old Major dies, the animals rebel and take over the farm. They rename it Animal Farm and create rules called the Seven Commandments.

At first, the animals believe they are building a fair society. The pigs, especially Napoleon and Snowball, become the leaders because they are considered the smartest.

Napoleon eventually uses dogs to chase Snowball away and takes full control. He slowly becomes more powerful and changes the rules to benefit himself.

The animals work harder and harder, but their lives do not improve. Boxer, the loyal horse, believes every problem can be solved by working harder.

Napoleon uses propaganda through Squealer to control the animals and convince them that everything is fine.

By the end, the pigs become just like the humans they replaced. The final message is that corrupt leaders can betray the people they claim to help.

👥 Main Characters

Old Major
The pig whose speech inspires the animals to rebel. He represents the ideas behind revolution.

Napoleon
A pig who becomes the dictator of Animal Farm. He represents corruption, dictatorship, and abuse of power.

Snowball
An intelligent pig who wants to improve the farm. He is forced to flee after Napoleon drives him away.

Squealer
Napoleon's spokesman. He spreads propaganda and convinces the animals that Napoleon is always right.

Boxer
A hardworking horse whose motto is "I will work harder." He represents loyal workers who are exploited.

Clover
A caring horse who notices that things are changing but cannot stop it.

Benjamin
An old donkey who is intelligent but rarely speaks. He understands what is happening but does little to stop it.

Mr. Jones
The original owner of Manor Farm whose poor leadership causes the rebellion.

💡 Major Themes

  • Power corrupts.
  • Propaganda and manipulation.
  • Equality vs. inequality.
  • Corruption of revolutions.
  • Leadership and dictatorship.
  • Education and ignorance.
  • Blind loyalty.

🎭 Important Symbols

The Farm
Represents Russia after the Russian Revolution.

The Seven Commandments
Represent laws that are slowly changed by those in power.

The Windmill
Represents hope, progress, and how leaders manipulate people through false promises.

The Dogs
Represent the secret police who keep dictators in power through fear.

Boxer
Represents hardworking people who are loyal but taken advantage of.

💬 Important Quotes

"All animals are equal, but some animals are more equal than others."

"Four legs good, two legs bad."

"I will work harder."

"Napoleon is always right."

📝 Essay Topics

  • How does power corrupt Napoleon?
  • Explain the role of propaganda in Animal Farm.
  • Why is Boxer one of the most tragic characters?
  • How does Orwell criticize totalitarian governments?
  • Explain the symbolism of the windmill.

⭐ Must Know Before the Test

  • Know each main character and what they represent.
  • Remember the Seven Commandments change throughout the story.
  • Understand propaganda and Squealer's role.
  • Know why Boxer is important.
  • Understand why the ending is ironic.

❓ Practice Questions

  1. Why do the animals rebel?
  2. Who becomes the leader after Snowball leaves?
  3. What does the windmill symbolize?
  4. Why is Boxer important?
  5. How does Squealer control the animals?
  6. What is the meaning of the final scene?
