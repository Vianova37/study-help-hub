🏠 A Doll's House

A Doll's House Study Guide

Act-by-act summaries, characters, themes, symbols, important quotes, essay help, and quiz.

⭐ Overview

Author: Henrik Ibsen

Published: 1879

Genre: Realistic Drama

Setting: The Helmer family's home in Norway.

Main Idea: Nora Helmer slowly realizes that she has lived her entire life being controlled by others. The play explores identity, marriage, independence, honesty, and gender roles.

👥 Main Characters

Nora Helmer
The main character. She appears cheerful and childish but becomes independent by the end.

Torvald Helmer
Nora's husband. He loves Nora but treats her like a child instead of an equal.

Nils Krogstad
A bank employee who knows Nora's secret and threatens to expose her.

Mrs. Kristine Linde
Nora's old friend. She encourages honesty and maturity.

Dr. Rank
A close family friend who secretly loves Nora and is dying from illness.

🎭 Major Themes

  • Identity
  • Marriage
  • Gender Roles
  • Freedom
  • Sacrifice
  • Honesty
  • Growing Up
  • Self-Discovery

🎭 Important Symbols

The Doll House
Represents Nora being treated like a doll instead of an independent adult.

The Christmas Tree
Represents Nora's happiness fading as her problems grow.

The Tarantella Dance
Represents Nora trying to distract Torvald while hiding the truth.

The Door Slam
Represents Nora choosing independence and a new beginning.

📚 Act 1 Summary

Nora returns home after Christmas shopping. Torvald teases her about spending money and calls her names like "little squirrel" and "little skylark."

Mrs. Linde visits Nora after many years. Nora proudly reveals that she secretly borrowed money years ago to save Torvald's life by taking him to Italy. She forged her father's signature because women could not legally borrow money without a man's permission.

Krogstad, who lent Nora the money, threatens to expose her forgery if she does not convince Torvald to let him keep his job at the bank.

Key Events

  • Nora's secret loan is revealed.
  • Krogstad begins blackmailing Nora.
  • Mrs. Linde returns.

Practice Questions

  1. Why did Nora borrow money?
  2. Why did Nora forge her father's signature?
  3. What does Krogstad want?
  4. Why is Nora afraid?

📚 Act 2 Summary

Nora becomes more nervous because Krogstad may reveal her secret. She tries to convince Torvald to let Krogstad keep his job, but Torvald refuses.

Nora practices the tarantella dance to distract Torvald and delay him from reading Krogstad's letter.

Dr. Rank tells Nora that he is dying and also reveals that he loves her. Nora realizes she cannot ask him for help.

Key Events

  • Nora tries to stop Torvald from firing Krogstad.
  • Krogstad leaves a letter exposing Nora's secret.
  • Nora uses the tarantella to distract Torvald.
  • Dr. Rank confesses his love for Nora.

📚 Act 3 Summary

Mrs. Linde talks to Krogstad and they decide to be honest with each other. Krogstad wants to take back his letter, but Mrs. Linde says Torvald should know the truth.

Torvald reads the letter and becomes angry. Instead of protecting Nora, he worries about his reputation.

After another letter arrives saying Krogstad will not expose them, Torvald forgives Nora. But Nora realizes Torvald does not truly respect her.

Nora decides to leave Torvald and her children so she can discover who she really is. The play ends with Nora slamming the door.

Key Events

  • Torvald discovers Nora's secret.
  • Torvald shows he cares more about reputation than Nora.
  • Krogstad forgives the debt.
  • Nora leaves to become independent.

💬 Important Quotes

"I have been your doll-wife, just as at home I was Papa's doll-child."

Meaning: Nora realizes that both her father and husband treated her like a toy instead of a real person.

"I must stand quite alone, if I am to understand myself and everything about me."

Meaning: Nora understands that she needs independence to grow.

"Before all else, I am a human being."

Meaning: Nora chooses her identity as a person over the roles society gives her.

📝 Essay Topics

  • How does Nora change throughout the play?
  • How does Ibsen criticize gender roles?
  • What does the title A Doll's House mean?
  • Why is the door slam important?
  • Is Torvald responsible for Nora leaving?

⭐ Must Know Before the Test

  • Nora borrowed money to save Torvald's life.
  • Nora forged her father's signature.
  • Krogstad blackmails Nora.
  • Torvald cares more about reputation than Nora's sacrifice.
  • Nora leaves because she wants independence and self-respect.
