🎭 Literary Devices

Metaphor: A comparison that says one thing is another.

Simile: A comparison using “like” or “as.”

Symbolism: An object, person, or event that represents a deeper meaning.

Irony: When reality is different from what is expected.

Foreshadowing: Hints about what will happen later.

Imagery: Descriptive language that appeals to the senses.

Allusion: A reference to a famous person, event, book, or idea.

📝 Reading Skills

Theme: The message or lesson of a story.

Conflict: The main problem in the story.

Characterization: How the author shows a character’s personality.

Setting: Where and when the story happens.

Tone: The author’s attitude.

Mood: The feeling the reader gets.

Point of View: Who is telling the story.
